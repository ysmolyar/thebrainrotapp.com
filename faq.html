What is brainrot? +

brainrot is a screen time control app that helps you reduce phone usage through a unique visual approach. It displays a brain avatar that deteriorates with excessive app usage, gamifying the process of reducing screen time. Users save an average of 1 hour 4 minutes daily.

How does brainrot protect my privacy? +

brainrot uses Apple's Screen Time API for monitoring and blocking. All processing runs entirely on your device - no browsing data ever leaves your phone. We take privacy seriously and don't collect or store your personal usage data.

I'm having issues blocking/unblocking apps +

First, try the refresh button at the top of the help page - this resolves 99% of blocking issues.

If that doesn't work:

  1. Turn it off and on again
    • Disable and re-enable rules in the rules tab
    • Close and reopen brainrot
    • Restart your device if needed
  2. Remove iOS limits or downtimes
    • Remove any Screen Time limits from iOS or other apps

  3. Force quit background apps
    • Memory limitations can cause issues
    • Quitting background apps helps clear memory

Why is my estimated screen time inaccurate? +

Screen time data relies on Apple's Screen Time system, which can sometimes have delays. Certain calculations, like brain health, rely on estimated screen time which can deviate from actual screen time. We strive for high accuracy but cannot guarantee 100% accuracy due to system limitations.

Why is my health score so low? +

Your health score is calculated based on your screen time compared to your set goal. Exceeding your daily goal lowers the score. Check the main view for a detailed breakdown of your usage and how it affects your brain health score.
